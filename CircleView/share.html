Take your DIY Spectroscopy to the next level!

Our friends at PublicLab have developed an online database of user-generated spectra and some fantastic free tools to analyze your samples!




NOTE: After you crop and calibrate your sample, SpectraSnapp saves that image to your phone or tablet's gallery. Most mobile web-browsers can't support the online tool, so you'll have to go into your phone's gallery, email yourself the cropped spectra image and then proceed on a desktop or laptop computer.

Once you have your spectra on your computer, visit http://www.spectralworkbench.org, and make a free profile. Drag down the arrow next to "Capture spectra" on the top right of the screen, then select "upload image"




Title your source, add any notes you may like, then hit "Choose File" to upload your sample. Make sure "vertical" is unchecked then hit upload.

In order to get accurate wavelength readings, you'll have to upload a spectra from a compact fluorescent bulb (CFL) and run their simple calibration tool. Spectral Workbench is under development, so keep adding and comparing your spectra to the database and look out for new analysis tools!


When sharing spectra on Facebook and Twitter, make sure to add #SpectraSnapp and follow @PhysicsCentral!
